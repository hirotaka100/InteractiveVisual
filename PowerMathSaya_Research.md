THE PROBLEM AND ITS BACKGROUND

Background of the Study

 	Mathematics is an essential subject that supports learning across many areas of life (Yves, 2022). It is present in everything the researchers do, from simple tasks to complex decisions (Gonzales, 2022). According to Gonzales (2024), mathematics plays a crucial role in the school curriculum as it is foundational for developing a wide range of skills. In elementary education, particular focus is placed on teaching the four basic operations, which are essential for building a solid understanding of numbers and their application in daily life. Mathematical thinking also plays a significant role in developing knowledge in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM). A lack of mathematical reasoning can hinder students’ success in higher education (Ferdianto, 2022).

	Numeracy, a critical competency, includes the ability to confidently identify and work with numbers, count, perform simple operations, and solve problems. These skills are necessary for understanding more complex mathematical concepts (Gonzales, 2024). Numeracy, as defined by Celemin (2023), is the ability to apply mathematical concepts to everyday situations.  This encompasses skills like counting, number recognition, basic arithmetic (addition, subtraction, etc.), problem-solving, sorting, observation, pattern identification, and more. Moreover, numeracy is the ability to apply simple numerical concept to the day-to-day activities of human (Abdulkareem, 2021). (Amora, 2024) postulate that learning mathematics and developing the necessary mathematical skills is integral to our daily lives. Furthermore, numerical skills are essential to make informed decisions in our daily life. Unfortunately, many people lack basic numeracy, which limits their ability to accurately interpret risks. (Retamero,2019). Studies have suggested that numeracy is correlated to financial outcomes, suggesting its relevance to financial decisions. (De Bruin,2021). 

	Despite government initiatives like Republic Act 10533 (Enhanced Basic Education Law) and Republic Act 10157 (Kindergarten Act) aimed at improving mathematics education in the Philippines, significant gaps remain.  This is evidenced by consistently low performance on national assessments (NAT) and international benchmarks like PISA and TIMSS.  For example, the Philippines scored among the lowest in mathematics in both the 2018 PISA assessment (second to lowest among 79 countries) and the 2019 TIMSS assessment (297 points, among the lowest of 58 participating countries).  This indicates a persistent need for improved mathematics education strategies.

	No exception to San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School’s Grade 3 learners showed a significant numeracy gap-90% requiring major support according to the Albay Numeracy Assessment Tools (ALNAT). Teachers noted that traditional methods of teaching were ineffective in engaging students, leading to low motivation and poor performance in mathematics. To bridge the gap in effective teaching strategies, the researcher found relevant studies addressing the problem. Patil (2021) highlighted that game-based interactive learning presentations significantly improve student understanding and test performance compared to traditional methods, making them an excellent tool for both teaching and learning. Nob et al. (2024) demonstrated that gamification in Philippine education fosters greater engagement, motivation, academic achievement, and higher-order thinking, especially when combined with competition, collaboration, rewards, and personalized feedback. In addition, Zubairu et al. (2021) emphasized the positive impact of game-based interactive learning materials, recommending the integration of game-based interactive learning materials packages in schools to enhance learning outcomes, particularly in civic education. Together, these studies underscore the transformative potential of combining interactive and gamified digital tools to enrich educational experiences and outcomes.

	Consequently, the researcher developed and evaluate "PowerMathSaya," a game-based interactive learning materials designed to improve the numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners. PowerMathSaya uses engaging games, visual aids, immediate feedback, tutorial video and rewards to make learning fun and effective. This study specifically targets improving the least-mastered numeracy competencies of Grade 3 students at San Isidro Talisay Elementary School.

Statement of the Problem

  	 This study aims to establish the effectiveness of the PowerMathSaya approach, which utilizes Game-Based interactive learning materials as an instructional strategy, in improving the numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners at San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School. Specifically, this study answers the following questions:

What is the level of ALNAT results of Grade 3 learners of San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School?

What intervention can be developed to enhance the level of numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners?

What is the numeracy level of Grade 3 learners of San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School after the implementation of PowerMathSaya?

Is there a significant difference in the level of numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners before and after the implementation of  PowerMathSaya?

What is the evaluation of teachers and experts on the developed intervention in terms of:

Content Quality

Instructional Quality

Technical Quality

What are the recommendations given by the evaluators to improve the intervention?

Hypothesis

	A significant difference was observed in Grade 3 students' ALNAT scores between pre- and post-intervention assessments using PowerMathSaya.

Scope and Delimitation

	This study seeks to improve the numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners at San Isidro Talisay Elementary School by using PowerMathSaya, a game-based, interactive learning approach. The research focuses on evaluating the effectiveness of these materials in improving learners’ numeracy performance. A total of 56 Grade 3 learners participated in this study. Additionally, 12 evaluators-composed of Mastered Teachers, Mathematics Teachers and IT expert-evaluators-assessed the content, appropriateness, and effectiveness of the PowerMathSaya materials to ensure their alignment with the K-12 Mathematics curriculum and their potential to support numeracy development. The study was conducted within the school setting over a period from February to March 2025.

	This study is limited to 56 Grade 3 learners at San Isidro Talisay Elementary School. It does not included learners from other grade levels or schools. Learners who were frequently absent or failed to consistently participate in the activities were not included in the study, as their limited exposure to the intervention could affect the accuracy and validity of the findings. The group of evaluators is restricted to the 12 individuals-comprising selected mastered teachers, mathematics teachers, and IT experts-who were available during the implementation period. The findings are confined to the specified timeframe, participant group, and school setting, and may not be generalized beyond this context without further study.

	The research gap identified in this study focuses on the lack of innovative and interactive technology-based teaching tools that particularly designed to improve numeracy skills of elementary students, especially in the context of Philippine education. While traditional teaching methods are still widely used, they often prove insufficient to engage students' interest, leading to poor motivation and suboptimal performance in mathematics.

	As such, this will be the first research investigating the relationship between implementing a game-based interactive learning material in enhancing numeracy skills in the Philippines, namely PowerMathSaya. It aims to make the learning process more enjoyable and accessible while children learn in a more effective way. It combines visual aids, variety of games, video tutorials to help learners better understand math concept, and immediate feedback that will encourage learner. This study addresses these gaps by developing and analyzing the PowerMathsaya program as a supplemental teaching tool to increase numeracy skills, enhance student engagement, and align teaching strategies with modern educational demands.

This informs the development of POWERMATHSAYA, which is rigorously evaluated for content, instructional, and technical quality before implementation. Definition of Terms

 	The researcher identified and defined important terms frequently used in the study to ensure clarity and consistency in understanding. These definitions were drawn from relevant literature and adapted to fit the context and scope of the research. 

	Numeracy. It is the ability to understa4nd and work with numbers (Merriam Webster, 2025). The ability to understand, interpret, and work with numbers in everyday context, such as solving problems, making decisions, and analyzing data. 

	Numeracy skills. Mathematical literacy, are an essential aspect of life that empowers individuals to interpret, analyze, and make sense of numerical information. These skills extend beyond basic arithmetic and are crucial in various aspects of daily life, education, career, and decision-making. (Valchev, 2025). The ability to use mathematical concepts such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division and data analysis to solve problem and make a decision in real-life situation. 

	Gamification. The application of game mechanics like point scoring, timing or competition to motivate engagement with a product or service. Injecting everyday tasks with a sense of play incentivizes people to not only complete them but to keep coming back for more (Koss, 2024). A strategy that integrates gameplay mechanics into activities or systems to enhance students experience and encourage desired behaviors, such as learning or productivity. 

	Game-based learning. Popular approach where the instrument for a learning process is a game. Game-based learning scenarios are often found online - they are often favored because they engage learners in a way that few other learning methods do (SlideLizard, n.d.). An instructional approach that uses game elements, such as challenges, rewards, and competition, to enhance student engagement and learning outcomes and a teaching method where educational content is integrated into games to promote active participation and skill development. 

	PowerMathSaya. PowerPoint-based educational tool that incorporates mathematics games and interactive activities to make learning math enjoyable. A digital resource designed to enhance math instruction through engaging and visually appealing multimedia content.

Chapter 3



RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

	This chapter presents the processes and procedures used in the development of the study. It includes sources of data, instrumentation, data gathering, sampling plan and statistical tools.

Methods of Research

	This study employed Quantitative-Descriptive-Developmental-Evaluative research design. The descriptive method used in describing the least mastered skills in Mathematics 3 based on the ALNAT results, detailed the content, skills, values, features, and functionalities of the developed game-based learning material. 

	While the developmental aspect involved creating this game-based learning material to address the identified skill gaps in Mathematics 3. The materials conceptual design focused on content quality, instructional, and technical quality. 

Finally, an evaluative approach assessed the materials alignment with the Learning Resource Management and Development System (LRMDS) guidelines for non-print materials and Expert’s Evaluation Based on ISO 9621-1. This evaluation process facilitated review, immediate feedback, and incorporation of evaluator recommendations for improvement.

Population, Sample, Size and Sampling

	The study population consisted of all fifty-six (56) Grade 3 learners of San Isidro Talisay Elementary School together with their advisers during the 2024-2025 school year. These learners were divided into two sections: Grade 3-Honest (25) and Grade 3-Patience (31).

	Additionally, 12 evaluators-composed of Mastered Teachers, Mathematics Teachers and IT expert-evaluators-assessed the content, appropriateness, and effectiveness of the PowerMathSaya materials to ensure their alignment with the K-12 Mathematics 3 curriculum and their potential to support numeracy development.

	Exclusion criteria included students with severe absences during the use of PowerMathSaya because their exposure to the intervention through PowerMathSaya would have been minimal, and students who did not exhibit consistent participation in classroom activities, as their irregular attendance and participation might affect the consistency of their responses. This non-probability sampling method was chosen due to the relatively small population size and the feasibility of including all members. No random sampling techniques were 

employed.

Description of the Respondents

	The non-probability sampling method was used in choosing respondents and evaluators in this study. No random sampling techniques were employed due to relatively small population size and respondents’ convenience.

	San Isidro Talisay Elementary School comprised the grade 3 respondents in the present study. To draw a convenience sampling. 56 students were selected because they were enrolled in mathematics classes during the research and have minimal absent, 25 students from Grade 3 Honest and 31 students from Grade 3 Patience to ensure an equal outcome. This sampling method provided a feasible and accessible group.

	For the evaluators, a purposive sampling was used. The total enumeration of ten (10) mathematics teachers, including two (2) advisers of Grade 3 respondents of San Isidro Talisay Elementary School. One (1) master teacher from Baldovino Elementary School teaching Grade 3 Mathematics and one (1) IT experts were sought to participate in the study by evaluating the technical quality of the developed PowerMathSaya game-based learning material. In all, twelve (12) evaluators evaluated the developed PowerMathSaya game-based learning material. The reduced sample was chosen based on some inclusion criteria to ensure relevance. These include a willingness to participate in evaluation, ability to express experiences with PowerMathSaya, and good representation across different levels of numeracy proficiency.

	The overall inclusion criteria for the study were that learners must be enrolled in the target Grade 3 Mathematics class. Exclusion criteria included learners with severe absences during the study because their exposure to the intervention in PowerMathSaya would have been minimal, and learners who did not exhibit consistent participation in classroom activities, as their irregular attendance and participation might affect the consistency of their responses.

	The evaluators of game-based learning material have knowledge on the competencies of Mathematics 3. They have also knowledge about technology game-based learning.

Data Gathering Procedure

	Data collection was based on four weeks. PowerMathSaya as an interactive learning tool was introduced to them and incorporated into their mathematics class. A posttest of the same format as the pretest (ALNAT) was given at the end of the fourth week to measure possible changes to the learner’s numeracy skills. The pretest and posttest results were compared to determine whether using PowerMathSaya improves their numeracy skills.

	The evaluation of PowerMathSaya was done face-to-face on the convenient of the evaluators wherein the researcher presented the features of PowerMathSaya as well as demonstrated how to use it, supported with user’s manual sent to the evaluators prior to the schedule of the evaluation.

	The overall study was in complete conformity with the highest standards of ethics. Prior to commencing data collection, the researchers obtained informed consent from all participants. Permission letters were distributed to the school principal, advisors, learners, parents/guardians and evaluators. These letters clearly outlined the study objectives, specifically detailing the role of PowerMathSaya in enhancing numeracy skills. Crucially, the letters emphasized participants’ right to withdraw from the study at any point without prejudice. Furthermore, the letters assured confidentiality, stating that all collected data would remain confidential and solely utilized for the study’s purposes. Each letter included contact information for addressing inquiries and required signatures to document consent, ensuring complete ethical compliance with established research protocols.

Research Instrument

	This study used a pretest (ALNAT) and posttest to measure numeracy skills, and an adapted LRMDS and ISO rating scale to evaluate the content, instructional design, technical quality, and overall effectiveness of the POWERMATHSAYA game-based learning material.  Evaluator scores determined whether POWERMATHSAYA met quality standards, with feedback informing further improvements.

	Meanwhile, the ALNAT (pre-test) was used to determine the needs of major support learners in Mathematics 3. The data were taken from the principal of San Isidro Talisay Elementary School.

Statistical Tool

	This study employed descriptive statistics, including frequency counts, percentages, weighted mean and rankings to analyze data from document analysis, and evaluation rating sheets.	

	The ALNAT (pre-test) was used to determine the needs of major support learners in Mathematics 3 to answer SOP number 1 of the study. The developed PowerMathSaya game-based learning material addressed SOP number 2. A post-test was conducted to answer SOP number 3, while a paired sample t-test was utilized to analyzed data for SOP number 4. A weighted mean was calculated to address SOP number 5. Question number 6 was answered using document analysis.

The analysis showed a non-significant weak positive correlation between the pretest and posttest scores, r (56) = .119, p = .382. This result indicates that individual learners’ relative performance on the pretest was not strongly predictive of their performance on the posttest. This may imply that the intervention had a uniformly positive effect, benefitting both lower- and higher- performing students prior to the intervention.

	The paired samples t-test showed that the mean difference between posttest and pretest score was statistically significant, t (55) = 33.96, p < .001. The mean difference was 19.14 with a 95% confidence interval ranging from 18.01 to 20.27, indicating a consistent and robust effect of the intervention. The relatively small standard error of the mean difference (SE = 0.56) further suggests a high precision of the estimated effect.

	Overall, these results provide compelling evidence that the PowrMathSaya program had a statistically and educationally significant impact on learners’ numeracy skills. Chapter 5

SUMMARY, CONCLUSIONS, RECOMMENDATIONS

	This chapter presents the summary of the findings on integrating game-based interactive learning material in Mathematics 3. The conclusions derived from the findings, as well as the recommendations were presented in this chapter.

Summary

	The study was conducted to develop and evaluate a game-based interactive learning material in Mathematics 3. The study delved on studying the least mastered skills presented by the results of the Albay Numeracy Assessment Tool (ALNAT) of Grade 3 pupils in the school of San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School. This was guided by the following specific objectives: determined the least mastered skills in Mathematics 3 in San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School; developed game-based interactive learning material designed to improve the least mastered learning competencies in Mathematics 3; identified content, skills, and values can be integrated into the game-based interactive learning material; determined the result of the evaluation of the evaluators on the developed game-based interactive learning material, in terms of Content Quality, Instructional Quality, Technical Quality; and Other Findings, as well as Functionality, Reliability, Usability, Efficiency, Maintability, Portability, and Compliance; and determined the recommendations given by the evaluators to enhance the developed game-based interactive learning material in Mathematics 3. 

	The study used Quantitative-Descriptive-Developmental-Evaluative method of research in gathering information to answer the specific questions in this study. The Post Test and evaluation rating sheet for non-print material were used as the main data gathering instruments. There were fifty-six (56) Grade 3 learners of San Isidro Talisay Elementary School during the 2024-2025 school year. These learners were divided into two sections: Grade 3-Honest (25) and Grade 3-Patience (31). There were twelve (12) evaluators who evaluated the develop game-based interactive learning material composed of Teachers, Master Teacher, and Information Technology Expert. 

Findings

	The following are the findings of the study on integrating game-based interactive learning material in Mathematics 3.

	1. The level of Albay Numeracy Assessment Tool (ALNAT) of Grade 3 pupils in the school of San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School revealed that all learners needed major support in developing their mathematical skills. All 56 learners (100%) fell under the “Needs Major Support” category, with scores ranging from 10% to 60%. The findings showed that none of the Grade 3 learners achieved full proficiency, with all scoring below the expected mastery level. This indicates that before the use of PowerMathSaya, learners had significant difficulties in fundamental mathematical operations and overall numeracy skills. 

	2. The developed game-based interactive learning material is named “PowerMathSaya” designed to address the least mastered learning competencies in Mathematics 3. Basically, it used Microsoft PowerPoint, a familiar and accessible platform that allows for full interactivity while remaining editable and free. It is complete with lively visuals, music, voice-over instructions, and point scoring to simulate the excitement of a televised game show. A tutorial video with voice-over was created to guide users on how to navigate, play, and customize the game.

	3. The numeracy level of Grade 3 learners of San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School after the implementation of PowerMathSaya revealed a significant improvement in students' numeracy skills. Through the use of PowerMathSaya, many students showed better understanding and application of mathematical concepts. There are only 4 learners (7.14%) remained in the “Needs Major Support” category. Meanwhile, 13 learners (23.21%) progressed to the “Emerging” level (70-79%). 15 learners (26.79%) reached the “Anchoring” level (75-89%), and 19 learners (33.93%) advanced to the “Developing” level (85-89%). Notably, 9 learners (16.07%) achieved the highest category of “Transforming” scoring 90% and above.  It indicates that the PowerMathSaya materials effectively helped enhance students' numeracy skills from the first to the third quarter. 

	4. There is a noticeable improvement in the ALNAT (Assessment of Learning Numeracy Assessment Tool) scores of Grade 3 students after using PowerMathSaya, showing a significant difference between their pre-test and post-test results. 

	5. The developed game-based PowerPoint learning material received a Very Satisfactory rating, exceeding the required passing scores in all areas evaluated. In terms of content quality, it earned a total score of 49.7 from teachers and a master teacher, surpassing the minimum required 40 points. Similarly, it received 49.8 for instructional quality and 64.6 for technical quality, both going well beyond the expected benchmarks. Under other findings, it achieved a perfect score of 16 out of 16.

	An Information Technology expert also evaluated the material and confirmed its strong technical performance. It met or exceeded all criteria, scoring 20 out of 20 in functionality, 15 out of 15 in reliability, and 20 out of 20 in usability. In terms of efficiency, it scored 10 out of the required 10 points. It also earned perfect scores in maintainability, portability, and compliance—each with 20 out of 20 or 5 out of 5, respectively. These results reflect the material’s overall quality, usability, and effectiveness in supporting student learning.

	6. There were ten (10) recommendations given by the evaluators. The evaluators offered a range of recommendations to enhance the game-based interactive learning material, focusing on both content and technical improvements. The recommendations given were: Incorporate real life scenarios and reflective questions or discussion at the end of the game, (2); Consider adding more examples or interactive elements, 1; The learning objectives must be clearly stated, 1; Turn up the volume of the voice over, 1; Break down complex concepts into smaller, more manageable steps, 1; Double check all hyperlinks to ensure they are working correctly, 1; Ensure that the slide are visually appealing with consistent formatting , 1; Add a timer if it will be used in classroom so that all problems can be answered, 1; Store it in a device with large storage because it tends to lag, 1; A tutorial should be provided on how this was made so that teachers can create their own without having to spend much, 1. These recommendations are aimed at improving the overall effectiveness, accessibility, and user-friendliness of the learning material for both students and teachers.

Conclusions

Based on the findings, the following are the conclusions drawn:

1. The ALNAT results for Grade 3 pupils at San Isidro, Talisay Elementary School indicated that all learners required major support in mathematical skills, highlighting a critical need for interventions to strengthen their fundamental numeracy abilities.

2. The development of "PowerMathSaya," a game-based interactive learning material, successfully produced an engaging, interactive, and accessible tool specifically designed to address the least mastered competencies in Grade 3 Mathematics.

3. After the implementation of PowerMathSaya, Grade 3 pupils showed significant improvement in their numeracy skills, demonstrating the material’s effectiveness in enhancing mathematical understanding and performance across the academic quarters.

	4. There was a clear and meaningful improvement in the ALNAT scores of Grade 3 learners after using PowerMathSaya, showing that the tool had a positive effect on their numeracy skills. 

	5. The evaluation results revealed that PowerMathSaya received a "Very Satisfactory" rating from both teachers and an IT expert, exceeding the required passing scores across all evaluated criteria, thus proving its high quality in terms of content, instruction, and technical standards.

	6. Evaluators provided ten (10) detailed recommendations aimed at enhancing PowerMathSaya’s content quality, technical performance, and overall usability, reflecting a commitment to further improve its educational impact and user experience.

Recommendations

	Based on the findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are humbly offered:

The teachers may sustain the integration of PowerMathSaya in daily Mathematics lessons to further strengthen the numeracy skills of Grade 3 learners.

The parents may help their children navigate the games interface and provide guidance on challenging problems %with the PowerMathSaya application, Grade 3 learners of San Isidro Talisay will receive support from their teachers, parents, or family members residing with them; for example

 Teachers may incorporate real-life scenarios, reflective questions, timers, and breaking down complex concepts into simpler steps to improve both engagement and understanding into the game-based interactive learning material.

Parents and teachers may address technical concerns, such as lagging, by ensuring the material is used on devices with sufficient storage and by optimizing file size when possible.

Future researchers may consider exploring improvements to PowerMathSaya, such as integrating more advanced gamification features, mobile app versions, or adaptive learning systems to cater to different learner needs.
